$$ \sigma_w^2 = 1.7u_*^2(1-z/z_i)^\frac{3}{2}\tag{ES9} $$$$ \sigma_u^2 = 4.0u_*^2(1-z/z_i)^\frac{3}{2}\tag{ES10} $$$$ \sigma_v^2 = 5.0u_*^2(1-z/z_i)^\frac{3}{2}\tag{ES11} $$

# initialize z
z = np.linspace(0, 1, 100)

# get sigma
sigma_w2 = 1.7 * ustar_n ** 2 * (1 - z / np.max(z)) ** (3 / 2)  # ES9
sigma_u2 = 4.0 * ustar_n ** 2 * (1 - z / np.max(z)) ** (3 / 2)  # ES10
sigma_v2 = 5.0 * ustar_n ** 2 * (1 - z / np.max(z)) ** (3 / 2)  # ES11

# plot it
plt.plot(sigma_w2, z, label="$\sigma_w^2$")
plt.plot(sigma_u2, z, label="$\sigma_u^2$")
plt.plot(sigma_v2, z, label="$\sigma_v^2$")

plt.ylabel("$z/z_i$")
plt.xlabel("velocity variance ($m^2/s^2$)")
plt.title("Turbulent Velocity Variance in the Stable/Neutral PBL")

plt.legend()

Discussion

In the absence of convection, we expect velocity variance to be generated mechanically from wind shear. Shear ($\frac{dv_i}{dx_i}$) is typically at a maximum near the surface, and fades as we approach the free atmosphere (constant wind). This parameterization has the smallest $\sigma$ value in z, as stable atmospheres supress vertical movement. We also expect more dispersion in the crosswind direction than in the downwind, consistent with this parameterization.

Discussion

For the free convective case, we expect the largest generation of buoyant turbulence production to occur some distance above the surface, where heat flux is large and large eddies are able to form. Near $z_i$, we observe a decrease in $\sigma_w$ where turbulence is being consumed by eating away at the BL inversion

Discussion

Turbulence near the surface should follow a "law of the wall" -- where the veloctiy of a fluid drops to zero near a rigid boundary. As we move away from the surface, larger eddies are able to form uninhibited by the surface, and so $\sigma$ increases.
